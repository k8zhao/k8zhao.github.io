FIXIT

This was a week-long personal design project inspired by my supervisors at the Cohon University Center who don't have the best communication with Facilities Management Services on campus. Maintaining efficient communication is critical in running the University Center smoothly. My peers also seem to have similar problems with the maintenance request process. I thought this would be a fun and relevant project to take on.

INITIAL RESEARCH

Problem Scope

When campus facilities issues are not adequately taken care of in a timely manner, it affects a students’ experience.

Secondary Research Insights

Through online research, I found that proper facilities management:

  • Creates a good learning environment for students
  • Attracts new teachers and students by presenting a clean and safe environment
  • Needs to incorporate convenient technology that students are familiar with

Competitive Analysis

PRIMARY RESEARCH

Research Objective

I wanted to have a clear understanding of how students and maintenance workers feel about the maintenance request process. I wanted to answer these questions:

  1. Would students like to be more directly involved in the maintenance request process?
  2. How can the current process be improved?
  3. How much does delayed maintenance upkeep really affect students’ everyday lives and experiences at school?

Research Methodology: User Interviews

I conducted 7 total user interviews - 4 students, 2 staff members, and 1 Facilities Management Services manager.

Narrowed Research Insights

I came out of the user interviews with a lot of different directions on how I could proceed, so I decided to narrow down my research insights to focus the solution.

  • Delays lead to lack of student interest and effort
  • Current platform is outdated for both parties
  • Unfamiliar locations delay maintenance
  • Poor communication leads to frustration

Storyboarding Methodology: Speed Dating

I storyboarded my top 4 concepts and got user feedback from 20 people to see which concepts I should move forward with.

When getting user feedback, questions I considered were:

  1. How does this solution improve their current state?
  2. Could they see themselves using it?
  3. What would they do differently?

If I were to continue working on this project, I would use these success metrics to measure the success of my design:

  • How fast a user is able submit a maintenance request
  • How easily a worker is able to find the location of the maintenance issue
  • How efficiently either parties are able to contact each other

FINAL THOUGHTS

There are no right answers, just well supported ones! I was anxious about continuing the process with the wrong solutions, but I know I chose wisely because I had so much research and user feedback backing my choice.


You don't know anything because you are not the user. My first design iteration made complete sense to me, but everyone I tested on had no idea how it worked. This experience just reaffirmed what I already knew - user testing is important!
